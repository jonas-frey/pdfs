What does the topos classify?

Observation: Cart\mathbf{Cart}Cart is a Lawvere theory. It's a super theory of known Lawvere theories:

L(Ring)⊆L(R-Alg)⊆L(C∞-Ring)⊆Cart=:L(RC-Alg)\mathrm L(\mathsf{Ring})\subseteq\mathrm L(\R\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}) \subseteq\mathrm L(C^\infty\text{-}\mathsf{Ring})\subseteq\mathbf{Cart} =:\mathrm L(\mathsf{RC\text{-}Alg})L(Ring)⊆L(R-Alg)⊆L(C∞-Ring)⊆Cart=:L(RC-Alg)

Call its models real-continuous algebras. So a real-continuous algebra is in particular an R\RR-algebra, but besides the polynomials it has nnn-ary operations for all continuous f:Rn→Rf:\R^n\to\Rf:Rn→R.

We write RCL=RC-Algop\mathbf{RCL}=\mathbf{RC\text{-}Alg}^{\mathbf{op}}RCL=RC-Algop for the formal dual of RC-Alg\mathbf{RC\text{-}Alg}RC-Alg and call its objects real-continuous loci.

The inclusion Cart⊆Top\mathbf{Cart}\subseteq\mathbf{Top}Cart⊆Top gives rise to a Galois connection

RC-Alg⇆Topop\mathbf{RC\text{-}Alg} \leftrightarrows \mathbf{Top}^{\mathbf{op}} RC-Alg⇆Topop

The functor LLL is fully faithful on realcompact spaces.

  • A realcompact space is a completely regular space which contains all the "real" points of its SC compactification (it is possible to associate some kind of residue fields to elements of the SC compactification).
  • A space is realcompact iff it's a closed subspace of some RI\R^IRI.
  • All "small" metric spaces are realcompact: specifically, (X,d)(X,d)(X,d) is realcompact whenever it has no closed discrete subspace of measurable cardinal.

  • Call rcalg AAA archimedean, if it validates

Prop:

  • finite open cover topology on Cart‾\overline{\mathbf{Cart}}Cart corresponds to local rcalgs
  • open cover topology on Cart‾\overline{\mathbf{Cart}}Cart corresponds to local archimedean rcalgs.
  • However, in presence of flatness, local algs are automatically archimedean (since open covers generate all covers)

Examples:
